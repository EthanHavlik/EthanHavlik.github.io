About me

I have been playing video games forever, and when the notion of working in the video game industry in some capacity was presented to me, I made that my long term goal. I am constantly learning about what goes into making a game, from the artwork to the code. My horizons are broadened in the short term, and I will gladly work anywhere that I can practice my programming skills as that is the route I have chosen.

I first got interested in programming in middle school, where I was exposed to things like Scratch and Code Combat. In high school, my interests shifted to graphic design; those assignments were more interesting, and the programming classes were too focused on websites for my taste. I went to college for graphic design at first, but I quickly grew a distaste for being forced to create art, so I circled back around to programming and picked up Python before studying for an Associate's Degree in Software Development at CWI.

My skills

In addition to learning the languages of front end development, I have experience using frameworks to jumpstart website projects and even create simple games. I used Bootstrap to create this website, but my personal favorite framework is React for its sheer potential for complex apps.

Collaborating with AI

One of the less conventional skills I have learned in college is how to leverage AI to quicken my workflow and further my understanding about programming. I have learned how to write prompts that get me the answers I need to write better code in much less time than I would otherwise.
